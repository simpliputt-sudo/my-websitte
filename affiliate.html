Important

Quick Disclaimer (AdSense-safe)

Affiliate marketing means recommending products/services using a tracked link. If someone buys through your link, you may earn a commission. Results are not guaranteed and depend on trust, traffic, and consistency.

⚠️ Avoid “get rich quick” claims. Build trust. Promote only what you understand.
Start Here

Where to Begin (Step-by-Step)

Step 1 — Pick ONE niche

  • Digital skills (courses/tools), tech gadgets, education, freelancing services, web hosting, etc.
  • Pick something you can post about consistently.

Step 2 — Choose your “traffic method”

  • Website: write helpful guides + reviews (best long-term)
  • TikTok/YouTube: short scripts that lead people to your site
  • WhatsApp: share new posts (do not spam)

Step 3 — Create 3 “starter pages” on your site

  • “Start Here: Affiliate Marketing for Beginners”
  • “Best Tools for Beginners (Honest Pros/Cons)”
  • “Avoiding Scams & Unrealistic Promises”

Step 4 — Join platforms (below) and apply to programs

Many networks approve you first, then you apply to individual brands inside the dashboard.

Step 5 — Add simple tracking

  • Use a spreadsheet: Date / Link / Page / Clicks / Sales / Notes
  • Always disclose affiliate links: “This post contains affiliate links…”

How to Sign Up

Sign-Up Checklist (Use This For Any Platform)

  • Have your website ready: at least 5–10 helpful posts/pages.
  • Have a clean “About” + “Contact” page: builds trust.
  • Use a professional email: like yourname@domain.com later (Gmail is fine to start).
  • Be honest: traffic sources, content niche, promotion method.
  • Add disclosures: “This post may contain affiliate links…”
Content That Converts

What to Post on Your Site (So People Actually Click)

  • Beginner guides: “How to start affiliate marketing in Ghana (no hype)”
  • Tool comparisons: “Canva vs alternatives (who should use what)”
  • Step-by-step tutorials: “How to build a portfolio website (Hostinger + templates)”
  • Curated lists: “Best online skills to learn in 2026 + course links”

Tip: Don’t paste copied descriptions from other sites. Summarize in your own words.
